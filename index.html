Artwork

Self-Portraits

Fragmented Reflection

"Fragmented Reflection" is a self-portrait in acrylic on four canvas pieces, meticulously combined with thread to symbolize the fragile yet enduring nature of personal identity.

Duality of Entropy

"Duality of Entropy" is a contemplative piece that juxtaposes the seemingly chaotic nature of life's unexpected events with the beauty that can emerge from them. The use of vibrant colors and expressive brushstrokes captures the emotional complexity of this duality, illustrating how moments of personal catastrophe can coexist with and even give rise to profound connections. This piece invites viewers to reflect on the transformative power of relationships and the unexpected blessings that can emerge from life's challenges.

Evident Marks

"Evident Marks" is an intimate acrylic painting that vividly captures scars in meticulous detail. This piece marks a significant point in my artistic journey by visually confronting the physical and emotional imprints of medical trauma. The careful attention to the textures and colors of the scars reveals a narrative of pain, healing, and resilience. Through this artwork, I’m exploring themes of vulnerability, self-acceptance, and the profound impact of body image. "Evident Marks" serves as a testament to the therapeutic potential of art in processing and reclaiming one's identity.

Closeness and Distance

"Closeness and Distance" captures the intricate emotions involved in forming social relationships amidst feelings of isolation from differing life experiences. Portrayed in acrylic on canvas, the piece features two figures against a vibrant, multicolored backdrop that represents the emotional terrain of their interactions. Although physically close, the figures exhibit a subtle emotional distance, illustrating the challenge of seeking intimacy in friendships while grappling with personal insecurities. The artwork delves into the delicate interplay between connection and separation, highlighting how personal histories and varying experiences can simultaneously alienate and unify. It invites reflection on the fragile equilibrium of building meaningful relationships in the face of individual traumas and differences.

Sculptures

BOOBY TRAP

"Booby Trap" is a sculptural commentary on dysphoria and dysmorphia, envisioned within a post-apocalyptic framework. This piece humorously yet poignantly subverts the typical function of bras, which are often designed to meet stringent beauty standards. By presenting disfigured and excessively modified bras encased in plaster, the sculpture challenges the norms of aesthetic appeal and the fashion industry's relentless consumerism. These bras, transformed into what resembles fossilized remains, symbolize the destructive cycle of mass production, waste, and exploitation of consumer insecurities. "Booby Trap" invites viewers to reflect on the profound environmental and psychological impacts of our consumption patterns, encapsulated in a future where such practices have left indelible marks on society.

Curvature of Discontent

"Curvature of Discontent" is a vibrant and texturally rich sculpture that captures the complex feelings of dysphoria through a series of plastered forms resembling dripping curves and voluptuous shapes. The cube, adorned with vivid colors and intertwined with strands of greenery, symbolizes the growth and transformation amidst feelings of grotesqueness often associated with body image. This piece confronts the harsh realities of beauty standards and the discomfort they can generate, especially when juxtaposed against the natural, unbounded growth represented by the organic elements. The sculpture invites viewers to reflect on the societal pressures of fashion and beauty, emphasizing the personal journey of acceptance and the evolving perception of self-worth beyond conventional aesthetics.

Growth Pills

"Growth Pills" is an evocative installation made up of interconnected prescription bottles, each uniquely adorned with vivid green patterns. The bottles represent the necessary yet conflicted reliance on medications to maintain functionality and improve quality of life. This work explores the dichotomy between reliance and resistance within the context of personal health and societal expectations. It delves into the complexities surrounding medical interventions, illustrating how they can both empower and challenge one's sense of self. By reflecting on the nuances of dependency, the installation prompts a broader dialogue about the implications of pharmaceuticals on individual identity and personal growth.

BUSH BOX

"BUSH BOX" features a wooden box adorned with moss and decorated with children's alphabet blocks, containing a painted representation of a nipple within. This piece explores themes of privacy, protection, and the often hidden aspects of personal identity. The external moss covering suggests growth and natural concealment, while the playful, educational blocks on the surface symbolize learning and development. Inside, the depiction of the nipple—a traditionally private and nurturing part of the body—highlights themes of vulnerability and intimacy. The contrast between the public facade and the intimate contents within the box invites reflection on the layers of self that individuals choose to reveal or conceal in different contexts. This sculpture serves as a metaphor for the personal spaces and boundaries that are navigated daily, emphasizing the importance of personal privacy in growth and self-discovery.

Hands, Shoulders, Tits, and Toes

"Hands, Shoulders, Tits, and Toes" is an interactive textile sculpture composed of various body parts—hands, shoulders, breasts, and feet—each crafted from fabric, sewn, stuffed, and mounted on a moss canvas with velcro. This allows the parts to be detached and rearranged, embodying the concept of modularity and the fluidity of identity. The piece humorously but insightfully explores my personal experiences with body dysphoria, specifically referencing the use of a skin graft from the shoulder as a facial patch. This work challenges traditional perceptions of body integrity and the fixed nature of physical form, reflecting on the ongoing adjustments and adaptations required in the journey towards self-acceptance. The ability to rearrange the components symbolizes control over one's body narrative, highlighting the transformative potential of art in exploring and accepting changeable aspects of self.

Other Works

Curvature of Identity

"Curvature of Identity" playfully combines visual and textual elements to explore the concept of identity beyond conventional norms. Featuring a juxtaposition of skeletal X-rays with sketches and the bold declaration "I'm so queer, not even my spine is straight," this artwork uses humor to challenge and redefine perceptions of normalcy. The piece highlights the fluidity and complexity of identity, embracing differences that are not immediately visible. Through this expressive fusion, it invites viewers to reflect on their own perceptions of identity and the societal frameworks that shape them.

Fragile Moments

"Fragile Moments" is a compelling acrylic painting that captures the transformation of a light bulb from intact to shattered. The artwork contrasts the pristine clarity of an unbroken bulb with its fragmented counterpart, illustrating the sudden and irreversible moment of breakage. This visual metaphor extends to the themes of vulnerability and the delicate balance of existence. The painting's vivid details and reflective shards invite viewers to ponder the transient nature of moments and the beauty found in their fragility. By highlighting the brief instant of transformation, the piece encourages a deeper appreciation of the ephemeral and often overlooked aspects of daily life and its unexpected disruptions.

Revolving Realities

"Revolving Realities" presents a compelling visual arrangement of pen on paper drawings affixed to a red vinyl backdrop, evoking the imagery of a spinning record. This artwork captures the simultaneous, ongoing lives as depicted through the figures. The circular motion implied by the record hints at the cyclical nature of daily routines and the interconnectedness of individual experiences within shared spaces. The placement of the figures around the circle suggests a dynamic, almost musical interaction between personal and professional worlds, reflecting on how each moment and interaction contributes to the overall harmony of life. The choice of red vinyl not only adds a striking visual contrast but also symbolizes the passion and energy driving each individual's day-to-day existence. This piece invites viewers to consider the rhythmic patterns of life and the roles individuals play in the broader composition of human experience.

Echoes of Self

"Echoes of Self" presents a compelling visual narrative through an overlay of sketches depicting the artist’s face emerging from a face patch, a poignant symbol of personal insecurities. This work captures the multiplicity of self-perception, where each line and contour reflects varying emotions and states of mind associated with body dysmorphia. The fragmented and overlapping faces create a sense of motion and transformation, illustrating the ongoing internal dialogue about identity and self-image. This piece invites viewers into a deeply personal exploration of the struggles and complexities inherent in viewing oneself through a lens distorted by personal fears and societal expectations.

Temporal Blur

"Temporal Blur" is an acrylic painting that delves into the complex experiences of dysphoria and dysmorphia through the lens of time and motion. The artwork features a soft, almost ethereal portrayal of a face, with layers and colors that flow and merge into one another, creating a sense of movement and transformation. This visual representation captures the fluidity of self-perception and the shifting landscapes of identity that fluctuate over time. The subtle distortions and blending hues emphasize the inner turmoil and confusion often associated with dysphoria and dysmorphia, while also conveying the transient nature of these experiences. The piece invites viewers to reflect on the continuous evolution of personal identity and the fleeting clarity between shifts in perception.
